# Manager brief — internal note

**From:** Product leadership
**To:** Product Engineer (you)
**Subject:** Framing the "team task tracking" opportunity

---

## Context

This client (Nova Studio) is our 3rd request this quarter for a "lightweight" task-tracking tool. We think there's a pattern: small teams (10-30 people) reject heavy tools (Jira, full Asana) but have a real need for visibility on the management side.

## What we expect from you

A first version (cupcake) that proves the value — not a full platform. We want to ship fast and learn.

## Business constraints

- **Deadline:** first shippable version in 6 weeks max.
- **Dual audience:** the team members (who execute) AND the manager/COO (who steers). Don't forget the second one — they're the one who pays.
- **Differentiator:** simplicity. If it's as complex as Jira, we've lost.
- **The real stake:** adoption. A tool the team doesn't open = failure, even if it's perfect.

## Open question we're sitting with

The client insists on the "dashboard" view for the COO. Is that the core of the value, or a nice-to-have that can wait for v2? To be challenged.

## What we do NOT want in v1

- Fine-grained permissions/roles
- Third-party integrations (Slack, email…)
- Sub-tasks, dependencies, Gantt charts

Keep it simple. Cupcake first.
