# Market study — summary

**Topic:** Task-tracking tools for small teams (10-30 people)
**Date:** Q2 2026 — illustrative data for the discovery

---

## The landscape

The market splits into three families:

**1. The heavyweights** (Jira, Asana, Monday)
- Very complete, very configurable.
- Recurring complaint from small teams: too complex, long learning curve, "we only use 10% of the features."
- Low adoption in teams < 30 people: the tool is imposed by management but shunned by the teams.

**2. The ultra-simple** (todo lists, Google Tasks, digital sticky notes)
- Easily adopted by individuals.
- But: no team visibility, no reporting for management. Everyone in their own corner.

**3. The recent hybrids** (Linear, Height…)
- Good simplicity/power trade-off, very popular with tech teams.
- But: built for product/dev teams, technical jargon, poorly suited to an agency or a non-tech team.

## The gap in the market

There's a missing tool for small **non-tech** teams that combines:
- the **simplicity** of a todo list (immediate adoption, zero training)
- with a **steering view** for the manager (which todo lists lack)

In other words: the simplicity of group 2 + the visibility of group 1, without the complexity.

## Signals

- Searches for "simple Jira alternative" are growing.
- Small teams cite **adoption** as the #1 criterion, ahead of features.
- Management cites **visibility** ("knowing where we stand without a meeting") as the main pain point.

## Key tension to resolve

Simplicity (for the team) **vs** visibility (for the manager). These two needs pull in opposite directions: the more reporting you add, the heavier it gets. The winning product finds the minimum amount of steering that satisfies the manager without discouraging the team.
